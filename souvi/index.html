Our Techniques

Four Bending Methods

We deploy the right bending technique for each geometry to achieve optimal quality and precision.

01

Rotary Draw Bending

The most precise method. Pipe is held by a clamp die and bent around a rotating bend die — producing tight-radius, consistent bends with minimal distortion. Ideal for seat frame main structures.

02

Compression Bending

The pipe is held stationary while a slide piece pushes around a stationary bend die. Cost-effective for simpler geometries and larger production volumes with moderate-radius bends.

03

Ram Bending

A ram presses the pipe over a radius block with counter-rollers supporting the sides. Well-suited for large-radius bends in structural tube components with high repeatability.

04

3-Roll Bending

Three rollers progressively form the pipe into curves. Particularly effective for arcs and circular bends used in seat frame back structures and large tubular rings.
